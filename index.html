About

Program leader.
People builder.
Problem solver.

"What I find most satisfying isn't the delivery. It's the thinking that happens before it. Working through the upstream and downstream impact of a decision, calibrating scope against constraints, figuring out how the pieces should come together before execution even begins. That part takes real, deliberate thought. And somewhere in that process, I almost always learn something from the people I'm working with: a perspective I hadn't considered, or a way of approaching a problem I hadn't tried."

I started my career much like anyone else in technology. What changed my path was a simpler pull: I was more excited by why something needed to be built than by building it. That question led me toward business stakeholders, delivery teams, and eventually, to program management.

Over 17 years, I've led digital transformation across Retail, Supply Chain, Logistics, Commerce, and Media. Not as a specialist in any one domain, but as someone who believes that the skills of a great program manager travel well across industries.

As COO of Headrun Technologies, I played a crucial role in building the team to 85 professionals, sustained client relationships for up to 15 years, and led compliance certifications (ISO 9001 and ISO 27001) that put governance at the center of how we work.

What motivates me every day is purpose: knowing the work of a project team will eventually impact many lives. My proudest achievements aren't projects. They are the people I've worked alongside, and what they've gone on to do.

My career has been built delivering enterprise-grade programs close to the business, as a trusted partner, often embedded in client teams and governance. The next step is to take that same accountability from the inside, shaping not just delivery but direction.

What I Bring

What I bring.
Every engagement.

01
People-First Leadership
Taking care of your team is what drives the best project outcomes, not the other way around. This has been my operating principle since my first management role. My advice to any aspiring PM: "Take care of the team, and they will deliver the best outcomes for the project." Over 17 years, I've worked alongside 100+ individuals across teams, clients, and geographies. What many of them have gone on to do is something I'm more proud of than any project I've delivered.
02
Cross-Functional Stakeholder Management
From coordinating 40+ onshore and offshore stakeholders at Tesco to managing distributed teams across India, to being invited into a client's own governance calls, working across geographies, time zones, and functions is a constant, comfortable thread in my career.
03
Leading Through Distance
When the pandemic hit in March 2020, we moved to fully remote almost overnight. The hardest part wasn't the tools. It was learning to ask explicitly for things you'd normally sense in a room: whether someone was stuck, struggling, or just quietly checking out. That period changed how I manage people. Today, distributed teams are something I'm genuinely comfortable with, not because I've read a framework about it, but because I've lived the learning curve.
